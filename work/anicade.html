Work About Resume
Concept Case Study – 2020
AniCade, a dynamic virtual environment for better online social interactions during COVID-19.
Role
UX/UI Designer
Human-Centered Design, Visual Design, Interaction Design, & Prototyping
Team
Hyewon Kim, UX/UI Designer
Katie Shum, UX Researcher
Jackson Rong, UX Researcher

Problem

The COVID-19 pandemic has forced us to sacrifice our social connections. There was a sense of loss for many students, as many of their experiences were taken away from them. As college students ourselves, my team deeply resonated with the feeling. So, we designed a solution that alleviates the negative effects of social isolation on student's well-being.

Solution

As we experience a fast decline in face-to-face interactions due to the pandemic, AniCade is a mobile game app that aims to encourage meaningful online interactions between friends.

Spontaneous interactions

Find friends who are online on the map and interact with them. You can chat, share music, send emojis, send them gifts, or play games together.

Synchronous conversations

AniCade's chatroom fosters synchronous and instantaneous conversations between friends. Just like in real life.

Customize your avatar

Choose your favorite animal and customize it. You can find and collect items on the map or purchase them using AniCade coins.

Process

Going into this, our hypothesis was that cultivating higher levels of physical and social presence in online environments can reduce the negative effects of social isolation.

Examining the landscape

To validate our hypothesis and identify the opportunity spaces, my team conducted a literature review and a week-long autobiographical design study using Club Penguin as our base system.

📒
Autobiographical design: "people build a system use it themselves, use this experience to learn about the design space, and evaluate and iterate based on their own experiences" (Sengers et al., 2013).

Literature Review

We focused on reviewing literature from areas that would help us contextualize the problem space a little more.

Coronavirus & mental health
Female gender, being a student, and having symptoms indicative of coronavirus were associated with higher levels of depression and anxiety (Asian Journal of Psychiatry, 2020).
Socialization & mental health
Studies support the idea that social interactions and activities play a crucial role in contributing to one's well-being and mental health  (Journal of Clinical Psychiatry, 2000).
Online socialization
Studies support the idea that social interactions and activities play a crucial role in contributing to one's well-being and mental health  (Journal of Clinical Psychiatry, 2000).
Online vs. offline socialization
Online socialization can enhance one's ability to connect with others – but offline, physical interactions are more capable of providing social support and alleviating loneliness (Journal of Social and Personal Relationships, 2013).

Autobiographical design study

Club Penguin allows players – as penguin avatars – to play games, interact with people across the world, and earn rewards among many other activities. This was the perfect system for testing our hypothesis, because it fosters physical and social presence in the digital world through its features. All team members played at least one hour for a week and took notes on our sessions.

Insights from our research

😌 Emotional differences
There was an increase in positive emotions after play sessions with friends.
🤝 Relationship differences with existing friends
There was a heightened sense of connection with friends through novel activities.
👋 Social interactions with existing and new friends
There were more opportunities to meet new people and playing with friends made it easier to interact with strangers.

Framing the solution

Design requirements

We wanted to make sure that each design decisions we made aligned with the insights that we gained from our research.

Developing the solution

Create a mobile multiplayer game that allows users to maintain and engage in meaningful interactions with friends, while providing opportunities to create new ties.

Design concept & sketches

Based on the solution we developed, I came up with three defining features for our design and began prototyping them.

Bringing the ideas into life

Spatial visualization of friends for physical proximity

Players only appear on the map if they are online. Here, they can interact with their friends, collect or purchase items, or play mini-games. I felt that a physical visualization of users could foster a sense of geographical proximity, which could encourage more meaningful interactions between users.

Meaningful interactions between friends

Players can send emojis and gifts to each other, play games together, and even share music. I wanted to recreate the sense of spontaneity that we feel in real-life (for example, when you bump into a friend on the streets) by limiting the access to these interactions only to players who are online.

Maintain & build social connections

Players can send emojis and gifts to each other, play games together, and even share music. I wanted to recreate the sense of spontaneity that we feel in real-life (for example, when you bump into a friend on the streets) by limiting the access to these interactions only to players who are online.

An actual 'room' for chatting

There are two types of mini-games players can play: mini-game heart and mini-game plus. Mini-game heart allows users to play games with their friends, while mini-game plus operates on a larger server, allowing them to play games with both friends and strangers.

A sense of escapism

Players play as animal avatars, which they can customize. They can change their avatars from a selection of animals we offer.

Conclusion & next steps

The coronavirus pandemic has fueled the acceleration of technological adoption, which will only continue to dominate our daily lives. As we reflected on our own experiences in the midst of this outbreak, we realized the need for solutions that prioritize social connections. When there is a shift in social structure and life, design opportunity spaces arise. Designing AniCade allowed me to think ahead about the potential opportunity spaces as we progress into a more digital world.

That said, there was a lack of exploration and iteration when designing this product. Conducting more higher-level explorations and design iterations would have allowed me to make better and more refined design decisions. I also felt that the autobiographical design approach created more room for limitations and biases. In the future, I would focus on conducting user research on a broader and deeper level, as well as on design explorations and iterations.
